Camera Angles

Camera angles affect the audience perception of action and characters. A camera angle can suggest size, status, authority, weakness and even fate.

Straight Angle

Straight Angles (or normal angles) comprises 90 percent filming. It approximates eye level and is also called the objective angle.

Low or Tilt-up Angle

A Low or Tilt-up Angle shows the power or dominance over other characters or situations. By moving the camera closer to the floor, the size,height and impact of the subject are increased. .

High or Tilt-down Angle

A high-angle shot is a cinematic technique where the camera looks down on the subject from a high angle and the point of focus often gets "swallowed up."

Over the shlouder Shot

An over the shoulder shot shows the person in the foreground dominating the person in the background.

Tilt Frame Angle

A Tilt Frame Angle shows the madness or craziness of a situation. The camera is rotated at 15 degrees for the duration of the shot.

POV Angle

A POV ( Point of view) also called the subjective angle , shows the point of view of a particular character.
